Organelles & Their Functions

Membrane-Bound Organelles

  • The nucleus contains the cell's DNA and controls gene expression and cell reproduction.
  • Mitochondria are the sites of cellular respiration and ATP production.
  • The endoplasmic reticulum is a network of membranes involved in protein and lipid synthesis.
  • The Golgi apparatus modifies, packages, and ships proteins received from the ER.
  • Lysosomes contain digestive enzymes that break down waste materials and cellular debris.
  • Peroxisomes contain enzymes that break down fatty acids and detoxify harmful substances.
  • Vacuoles are membrane-bound storage compartments that maintain turgor pressure in plant cells.

    • Plant-Specific Organelles

  • Chloroplasts are the sites of photosynthesis in plant cells, containing chlorophyll.
  • The cell wall provides structural support and protection in plant, fungal, and bacterial cells.

    • Non-Membrane Bound Structures

  • Ribosomes are the sites of protein synthesis, found free in cytoplasm or attached to ER.
  • The cytoskeleton provides structural support and maintains cell shape through protein filaments.
  • Centrosomes are involved in organizing microtubules and are critical for cell division.
  • Microtubules are dynamic structures that can rapidly grow and shrink, allowing for changes in cell shape and movement. Microfilaments are thin protein fibers that support cell shape and are involved in muscle contraction and cell motility.
  • Centrioles organize microtubules and are involved in cell division and ciliary formation.
  • Intermediate filaments provide mechanical support and maintain cell shape, anchoring organelles in place.
  • The nucleolus is the site of ribosomal RNA synthesis and ribosome assembly within the nucleus.

    • Specialized Organelle Functions

  • Smooth ER synthesizes lipids and metabolizes carbohydrates, lacking ribosomes on its surface.
  • Rough ER has ribosomes attached and is involved in protein synthesis and processing. It contains a double membrane structure that folds proteins into their functional shapes.
  • Cristae are the folded inner membranes of mitochondria that increase surface area for ATP synthesis.
  • The Matrix is the innermost compartment of mitochondria where the citric acid cycle occurs.
  • Compartmentalization allows for the storage of metabolic intermediates and the separation of metabolic processes.

    • Organelle Interactions

  • The endomembrane system includes ER, Golgi, lysosomes, and vacuoles working together.
  • Vesicles transport materials between organelles and to the cell surface.
  • Endocytosis brings materials into the cell by forming vesicles from the plasma membrane.
  • Exocytosis releases materials from the cell by fusing vesicles with the plasma membrane.
