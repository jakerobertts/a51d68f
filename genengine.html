Genetic Engineering

  • Gene cloning involves isolating a specific gene and inserting it into a vector, such as a plasmid, to produce multiple copies of the gene.
  • Restriction enzymes are used to cut DNA at specific sequences, allowing for the insertion of foreign DNA into plasmids or other vectors.
  • Polymerase chain reaction (PCR) amplifies specific DNA sequences, enabling the production of large quantities of DNA for analysis or cloning.
  • DNA libraries are collections of cloned DNA fragments that represent the entire genome of an organism, used for sequencing and analysis.
  • cDNA is generated from mRNA using reverse transcriptase, allowing for the study of gene expression and the production of recombinant proteins.
  • CRISPR-Cas9 is a powerful tool for gene editing that allows for precise modifications to the genome by targeting specific DNA sequences.
  • Hybridization techniques, such as Southern blotting, are used to detect specific DNA sequences within a complex mixture, enabling the identification of genes or mutations.

    • Dive deeper: First, restriction enzymes are used to cut DNA at specific sequences, allowing for the insertion of foreign DNA into vectors. DNA ligase joins sticky ends at the backbones of DNA. Next, the recombinant DNA is introduced into host cells through transformation or transfection, enabling the expression of the inserted gene. Successful clones are selected via antibiotic resistance.
