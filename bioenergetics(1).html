Bioenergetics & Thermodynamics

Laws of Thermodynamics

  • The first law states that energy cannot be created or destroyed, only converted from one form to another.
  • The second law states that entropy of the universe always increases.

Free Energy

  • Gibbs free energy determines whether a reaction is spontaneous or non-spontaneous.
  • Exergonic reactions release energy (ŒîG < 0), while endergonic reactions require energy input (ŒîG > 0).
  • ATP hydrolysis provides energy for endergonic reactions through coupled reactions.
  • Le Chatelier's principle states that if a system at equilibrium is disturbed, it will shift to counteract the disturbance.
  • Concentration changes can affect the position of equilibrium, favoring the side with fewer moles of gas.
