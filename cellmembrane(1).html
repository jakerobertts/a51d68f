Cell Membrane Structure & Function

Membrane Structure

  • The fluid mosaic model describes cell membranes as a phospholipid bilayer with embedded proteins.
  • Phospholipids have hydrophilic heads and hydrophobic tails.
  • Cholesterol regulates membrane fluidity and is found in animal cell membranes.

Membrane Proteins

  • Integral proteins span the membrane and include transmembrane proteins and lipid-anchored proteins.
  • Peripheral proteins are attached to the membrane surface and can be easily removed.
  • Functions include transport, signaling, and catalysis.
  • Channels passively facilitate the movement of ions and small molecules across the membrane.
  • Receptors bind to specific ligands, triggering cellular responses.
  • Pumps actively transport substances against their concentration gradient.

Membrane Functions

  • The cell membrane acts as a barrier to separate the internal environment from the external environment, maintaining homeostasis.
  • Osmosis and diffusion are key processes for transporting and involve a downhill concentration gradient.
  • hypertonic, hypotonic, and isotonic solutions affect cell volume and pressure.
  • Membrane proteins facilitate transport of molecules across the membrane, including active transport and passive transport.
  • The membrane is selectively permeable, allowing certain substances to pass while restricting others.
