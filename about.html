Creator of MCATable

While exploring opportunities in the medical and biological sciences, I began preparing for the MCAT in May 2025. During that process, I identified a need for more effective, structured, and accessible MCAT review tools—especially those that emphasize active recall and concept integration. This platform is designed to support students in mastering both:
• Skill 1: Content knowledge and understanding
• Skill 2: Application and reasoning based on that knowledge
MCAT-able provides:
• Over 2,000 fill-in-the-blank questions aligned with AAMC content categories
• Immediate feedback to promote active learning and retention
• Progress tracking and analytics to help identify areas for improvement
My goal is to offer a high-quality, evidence-based resource for students preparing for one of the most rigorous exams in the pre-medical track.
