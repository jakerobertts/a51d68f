Biology - Cardiovascular System Concepts - MCAT Practice Questions

Which part of the electrical conduction system of the heart electrically connects the atria and ventricles and coordinates the top of the heart?
Atrioventricular (AV) node






The Bundle of His transmits electrical impulses directly from the:
AV node to the ventricles






What are the blood vessels that carry the contraction impulse from both the left and right bundle branch to the myocardium of the ventricles?
Purkinje fibers






Blood vessels that carry blood away from the heart are called:
Arteries






Which type of blood vessel is characterized by thin walls with few elastic fibers and carries blood toward the heart?
Veins






Tiny branches of arteries that lead to capillaries are known as:
Arterioles






Which blood vessels are only 1 cell thick and make up the endothelial wall, allowing for easy diffusion of gases and waste?
Capillaries






Antidiuretic Hormone (ADH) promotes:
Increased water retention and decreased plasma osmolarity






If your blood osmolarity is higher than normal, your body reacts to replace this water loss by releasing:
ADH






The cardiac output equation is:
Cardiac Output = Heart Rate × Stroke Volume






Blood is considered a/an:
Connective tissue






Red blood cells are also known as:
Erythrocytes






What is the term for the percentage of blood composed of RBCs?
Hematocrit






White blood cells are also known as:
Leukocytes






RBCs and WBCs are both formed in the:
Bone marrow






The process by which stem cells from bone marrow develop into mature blood cells is called:
Hematopoiesis






Which of the following is not a type of granulocyte?
Lymphocyte






Platelets, also called thrombocytes, function primarily to:
Initiate a blood clot by clumping






Which type of pressure moves fluid out of a capillary and into the interstitial fluid?
Hydrostatic pressure






The binding of oxygen to hemoglobin is characterized as:
Cooperative






CO₂ and H⁺ (acidity) will trigger the heme group to:
Release its O₂






A low pH (high H⁺ concentration) causes the oxygen saturation curve to shift right, which means a:
Low heme affinity for O₂






The molecule 2,3-BPG causes heme to:
Release O₂






Myoglobin stores and releases oxygen in:
Muscle tissue






The myoglobin oxygen saturation curve is hyperbolic because:
It only has one heme group and cannot exhibit cooperative binding
