Oxidative Stress & the Mitochondria

  • Oxidative stress occurs when there is an imbalance between the production of reactive oxygen species (ROS) and the body's ability to detoxify them, leading to potential damage to cells and tissues.
  • The mitochondria are a major source of ROS, particularly during electron transport chain activity, where electrons can leak and react with oxygen to form superoxide.
  • Peroxisomes are the primary organelles responsible for detoxifying ROS, converting them into less harmful substances.
  • Antioxidants are molecules that neutralize ROS, preventing oxidative damage.
  • Apoptosis occurs when cellular damage to mitochondria is severe and irreparable.
