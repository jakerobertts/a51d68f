About This Site

The MCAT is a standardized exam to get into medical school. It is used to assess the knowledge and skills of prospective medical students. This site provides a comprehensive overview of key concepts and topics relevant to the MCAT, including biology, chemistry, physics, psychology, and sociology. There are four main skills needed for the MCAT: content knowledge, problem-solving, data analysis, and critical thinking. The site is designed to help students prepare for the exam by providing clear explanations, interactive quizzes, and practice questions. The MCAT is extremely extensive. This site provides a condensed guide to Skills 1 and 2 (content knowledge and problem-solving) to help students focus on the most important concepts. Thus, data analysis and critical thinking are limited on this site.

The site is organized into sections based on the MCAT content categories, with each section containing detailed explanations, diagrams, and interactive quizzes to reinforce learning. The goal is to provide a comprehensive yet accessible resource for students preparing for the MCAT. The B/B section is the most extensive and covers a wide range of topics in biology and biochemistry, including cell biology, genetics, molecular biology, and physiology. The C/P section covers chemistry and physics concepts relevant to the MCAT, including chemical reactions, thermodynamics, and electromagnetism. Thus, these sections are broken up into several subtopic pages. The P/S section focuses on psychology and sociology concepts, including social behavior, cognition, and mental processes.
