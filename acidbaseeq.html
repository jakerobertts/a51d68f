Comprehensive MCAT Checklist - Acid and Base Equilibria

_________________________________________________________________________________
1. Which of the following best describes the difference between the Bronsted-Lowry and Lewis definitions of acids and bases?
Bronsted-Lowry focuses on proton transfer, while Lewis focuses on electron pair transfer






_________________________________________________________________________________
2. In the reaction: NH3 + H2O ⇌ NH4+ + OH-, is H2O acting as a Lewis acid?
Yes, because it accepts a pair of electrons from NH3






_________________________________________________________________________________
3. A molecule that LACKS a full octet is most likely to behave as:
A Lewis acid






_________________________________________________________________________________
4. Which of the following is false regarding the Bronsted-Lowry definition of acids and bases?
Acids do not need to have a hydrogen atom






_________________________________________________________________________________
5. Which of the following acids is most likely to act as a buffer in a solution with a pH of 7?
A weak acid with a pKa close to 7






_________________________________________________________________________________
6. Why do solutions that act as a buffer contain a weak acid or base?
Because weak acids and bases only partially dissociate, allowing them to neutralize added acids or bases without drastically changing the pH






_________________________________________________________________________________
7. Which process occurs when adding a strong acid or base to a buffer?
Neutralization, where the buffer components react with the added acid or base to minimize pH change






_________________________________________________________________________________
8. In the equation \( K_a = \frac{[H^+][A^-]}{[HA]} \), which of the following best describes the representation of each term?
Ka is the acid dissociation constant, [H+] is the concentration of protons, [A-] is the concentration of the conjugate base, and [HA] is the concentration of the acid






_________________________________________________________________________________
9. The pH is:
-log[H+]






_________________________________________________________________________________
10. If the pH of a solution is 3, what is the concentration of H+ ions?
1 x 10^-3 M






_________________________________________________________________________________
11. A large Ka value indicates:
A strong acid that favors the formation of products






_________________________________________________________________________________
12. Complete ionization (dissociation) means:
Molecules that give/accept protons completely






_________________________________________________________________________________
13. Water's autoionization constant (Kw = 10^-14 at 25°C) means:
At 25°C, the product of the concentrations of H+ and OH- ions in water is always 10^-14






_________________________________________________________________________________
14. Which of the following is NOT a strong acid?
CH3COOH






_________________________________________________________________________________
15. Monoprotic and diprotic acids are determined by their ability to:
Donate a certain number of protons






_________________________________________________________________________________
16. What is the resulting pH of a Ca(OH)2 dissociation in water with a concentration of 0.01 M?
12.3






_________________________________________________________________________________
17. Which best describes the dissociation of alkali and alkaline-earth hydroxides in water?
They dissociate completely, making them strong bases






_________________________________________________________________________________
18. Which of the following reactants cannot hydrolyze (react with water)?
NaCl, because it does not produce H+ or OH- ions






_________________________________________________________________________________
19. Although iodine is in the same group as fluorine, HI is a much stronger acid than HF. This is because:
The H-I bond is longer and weaker than the H-F bond, making it easier to dissociate
