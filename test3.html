Passage 1 (Questions 1‑5)

Sickle cell anemia is a genetic blood disorder that causes red blood cells to become abnormally shaped, like sickles. These sickle cells can get stuck in small blood vessels, leading to pain, organ damage, and other complications. At the biomolecular level, sickle cell anemia is caused by a mutation in the HBB gene, which provides instructions for making a component of hemoglobin. This mutation results in the substitution for glutamic acid in the beta-globin chain of hemoglobin.
