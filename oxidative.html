Oxidative Phosphorylation

Overview

  • Oxidative phosphorylation is the process of ATP synthesis driven by electron transport through the respiratory chain.
  • It occurs in the inner mitochondrial membrane of eukaryotic cells.

Practice Questions

Components

  • Complex I (NADH dehydrogenase.oxidizes NADH and pumps protons.
  • Complex II (succinate dehydrogenase.oxidizes FADH₂ without pumping protons.
  • Complex III (cytochrome bc₁) transfers electrons and pumps protons.
  • Complex IV (cytochrome oxidase.reduces oxygen to water.
  • ATP synthase uses the proton gradient to synthesize ATP.

Energy Yield

  • Each NADH yields approximately 2.5 ATP.
  • Each FADH₂ yields approximately 1.5 ATP.
  • The total yield from one glucose molecule (10 NADH and 2 FADH₂) is approximately 30-32 ATP.
