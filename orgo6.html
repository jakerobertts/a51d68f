Organic Chemistry - Separation Techniques - MCAT Practice Questions

What separation technique combines two immiscible liquids, one of which easily dissolves the compound of interest?
Extraction






In an extraction, what term describes the solid isolated during filtration?
Residue






Which layer in an extraction is the nonpolar, organic layer?
Nonpolar layer






If a liquid's boiling point is 160°C, which type of distillation should be used to prevent degradation of the product?
Vacuum distillation






What is the main advantage of using vacuum filtration over gravity filtration?
It is faster






Simple distillation can be used if the boiling points of the liquids are at least how many degrees Celsius apart?
25°C






What is the purpose of the vacuum in vacuum distillation (in terms of air pressure and boiling point)?
It lowers air pressure, decreasing the BP






Fractional distillation is used to separate components in a mixture according to their different boiling points when the difference is relatively small (less than 25°C).
True






Recrystallization is also known by what other name?
Fractional crystallization






A wash is described as the reverse of which major separation technique?
Extraction






Chromatography is a laboratory technique for the separation of a mixture based on what main factor?
How fast each component travels through a structure






In a typical chromatography setup, the stationary phase is generally polar or nonpolar?
Polar






In a typical chromatography setup, the mobile phase is generally polar or nonpolar?
Nonpolar






In chromatography, which type of molecules (polar or nonpolar) elute faster if the stationary phase is polar?
Nonpolar molecules






What is the ratio that defines the retention factor (Rf) in thin-layer chromatography (TLC)?
Distance the spot moved / Distance the solvent front moved






In a typical thin-layer chromatography (TLC) setup, which compound moves the shortest distance: the polar or the nonpolar substance?
The polar substance






What common substance is the stationary phase typically made of in TLC?
Silica gel






Gel-filtration chromatography separates molecules based on what physical property?
Size






In size-exclusion chromatography, which size molecules elute first: large or small?
Large






Ion exchange chromatography separates proteins based on their net charge.
True






High-performance liquid chromatography (HPLC) relies on what mechanical component to pass the solvent through the column at high pressure?
Pumps






Gas chromatography separates molecules based on their polarity and what other physical property?
Boiling point






In anion exchange chromatography, are the beads positively or negatively charged?
Positively charged






Affinity chromatography separates proteins based on their affinity for what specific component?
A specific ligand






In cation exchange chromatography, which type of proteins (positively or negatively charged) elute first?
Negatively charged proteins
