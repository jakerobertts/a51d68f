Psychology - Social Psychology - MCAT Practice Questions

Which term describes the tendency of people to perform at a different level when others are around?
Social Facilitation






A loss of self-awareness in large groups is known as:
Deindividuation






The phenomenon where individuals are less likely to respond to a person in need when others are present is called the:
Bystander Effect






What is the term for the social influence placed on individuals by others they consider equals?
Peer Pressure






When an individual does not pull his or her weight in a group setting, this is referred to as:
Social Loafing






The tendency toward making decisions in a group that are more extreme than the initial inclinations of its members is known as:
Polarization






Which tendency involves groups making decisions based on ideas that arise within the group without considering outside ideas?
Groupthink






The beliefs, ideas, behaviors, actions, and characteristics of a group or society are collectively described as:
Culture






The process by which an immigrant or minority takes up elements of the mainstream culture is called:
Assimilation






The encouragement of multiple cultures within a community to enhance diversity is best described as:
Multiculturalism






What is a group of people within a culture that distinguish themselves from the primary culture?
Subculture






According to the provided text, which of the following is NOT one of the three components of an attitude?
Structural






Which theory suggests that beliefs and attitudes are influential to various psychological functions?
Functional Attitudes Theory






The theory that states behavior is learned through watching and replicating others is the:
Observational Learning Theory






Which theory states that attitudes are formed through watching others, personal factors, and the environment?
Social Cognitive Theory
