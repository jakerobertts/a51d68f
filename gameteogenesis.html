Gameteogenesis & Fertilization

Spermatogenesis

  • Spermatogenesis is the process of sperm cell development in the seminiferous tubules.
  • Spermatogonia are diploid germ cells that serve as the stem cells for sperm production.
  • Spermatocytes undergo meiosis to reduce chromosome number from diploid to haploid.
  • Spermatids are haploid cells that differentiate into mature sperm through spermiogenesis.
  • Sertoli cells provide structural support and nutrients to developing sperm cells.

    • Oogenesis

  • Oogenesis is the process of egg cell development in the ovaries.
  • Oogonia are diploid germ cells that develop into primary oocytes during fetal development.
  • Primary oocytes are arrested in prophase I of meiosis until ovulation.
  • Secondary oocytes are arrested in metaphase II and released during ovulation.
  • Polar bodies are small cells formed during oogenesis that eventually degenerate.

    • Hormonal Control

  • FSH stimulates gamete production in both males and females.
  • LH triggers ovulation in females and testosterone production in males.
  • GnRH is released by the hypothalamus to control FSH and LH secretion.
  • Testosterone is essential for spermatogenesis and male sexual development.
  • Estrogen regulates the female reproductive cycle and oocyte maturation.

    • Ovarian Cycle

  • Follicular phase is when follicles develop and the primary oocyte matures.
  • Ovulation is the release of a secondary oocyte from the ovarian follicle.
  • Luteal phase is when the corpus luteum forms and secretes progesterone.
  • Corpus luteum is the structure that forms after ovulation and produces hormones.
  • Menstruation is the shedding of the endometrial lining when pregnancy doesn't occur.

    • Fertilization

  • Capacitation is the process that enables sperm to fertilize an egg.
  • Acrosome reaction is the release of enzymes that help sperm penetrate the egg.
  • Zona pellucida is the glycoprotein layer surrounding the egg that sperm must penetrate.
  • Cortical reaction prevents multiple sperm from fertilizing the same egg.
  • Zygote is the diploid cell formed by the fusion of sperm and egg nuclei.

    • Early Development

  • Cleavage consists of rapid cell divisions without growth in overall size.
  • Morula is a solid ball of cells formed after several cleavage divisions.
  • Blastula is a hollow ball of cells formed during early embryonic development.
  • Implantation is the attachment of the blastocyst to the uterine wall.
  • Placenta is the organ that allows nutrient and waste exchange between mother and fetus.
