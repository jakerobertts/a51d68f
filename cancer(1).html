Cancer Biology

Cancer Fundamentals

  • Oncogenesis is the process by which normal cells transform into cancer cells.
  • Neoplasm is an abnormal mass of tissue that grows independently of normal growth controls.
  • Malignant tumors can invade surrounding tissues and metastasize to distant sites.
  • Benign tumors grow locally but do not invade surrounding tissues or metastasize.
  • Metastasis is the spread of cancer cells from the primary tumor to distant sites.

    • Oncogenes and Tumor Suppressors

  • Oncogenes are mutated or overexpressed genes that promote cell proliferation and cancer.
  • Proto-oncogenes are normal genes that regulate cell growth and can become oncogenes when mutated.
  • Tumor suppressor genes normally prevent cancer by controlling cell division and promoting apoptosis.
  • p53 is a crucial tumor suppressor that responds to DNA damage by stopping cell division.
  • Rb is a tumor suppressor that controls the G1/S checkpoint in the cell cycle.
