Metabolic Pathway Regulation

  • Metabolic pathways are regulated by various mechanisms to maintain homeostasis and respond to cellular needs. Homeostasis that is maintained for a prolonged period is called a steady state.
  • Glycolysis, the citric acid cycle, and oxidative phosphorylation are key metabolic pathways that are tightly regulated.
  • Enzymes in these pathways can be regulated by allosteric effectors, covalent modifications, and feedback inhibition.
  • Hormones such as insulin and glucagon play crucial roles in regulating glucose metabolism and energy balance in the body.
  • Insulin promotes glucose uptake and storage, while glucagon stimulates gluconeogenesis and glycogenolysis to increase blood glucose levels.
  • They are both produced by the pancreas and have opposing effects on glucose metabolism.
