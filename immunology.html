Immunology

Innate Immunity

  • Innate immunity provides immediate, non-specific protection against pathogens.
  • Components include barriers, phagocytes, and complement system.

Adaptive Immunity

  • Adaptive immunity provides specific, long-lasting protection with memory.
  • B cells produce antibodies for humoral immunity.
  • T cells include helper T cells and cytotoxic T cells.
  • The MHC (Major Histocompatibility Complex) is essential for T cell recognition of antigens. Presentation of antigens on MHC molecules is crucial for T cell activation. MHC Class I presents to cytotoxic T cells (CD8+), while MHC Class II presents to helper T cells (CD4+).

Antibodies

  • The variable region binds antigens, while the constant region determines function.
