Sex-Linked Characteristics

  • X-inactivation is a process where one of the two X chromosomes in female mammals is randomly inactivated, leading to dosage compensation between males and females.
  • Males are more likely to express recessive traits linked to the X chromosome because they have only one X chromosome, while females have two and can be carriers without expressing the trait.
  • Sex is determined by X and Y chromosomes, with males having one X and one Y chromosome, and females having two X chromosomes.
  • There are very few genes on the Y chromosome, which is why Y-linked traits are much rarer than X-linked traits.
