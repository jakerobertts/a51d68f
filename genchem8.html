General Chemistry - Oxidation & Reduction - MCAT Practice Questions

What is the oxidation number of sulfur (S) in the sulfate ion, SO₄²⁻?
+6






What is the oxidation number of a neutral element, such as Fe or O₂?
0






According to the rules, what is the standard oxidation state for Group 2A metals, like Calcium (Ca), when it is in a compound?
+2






In which of the following compounds does Hydrogen (H) have an oxidation state of -1?
LiH (A metal hydride)






What is the oxidation state of Oxygen (O) in a peroxide, such as H₂O₂?
-1






When is a Group 7A metal, such as Chlorine (Cl), NOT assigned an oxidation state of -1 in a compound?
When it is combined with an element of greater electronegativity (e.g., O or F)






What must the sum of the oxidation numbers for all atoms in a neutral compound, like H₂O, equal?
Zero






What is the oxidation number of a monatomic ion, such as Mg²⁺?
Equal to the charge of the ion






Which type of equation includes all ions present in a reaction, including the spectator ions?
Complete ionic equation






Which equation lists only the species that are directly involved in the electron transfer or reaction and excludes spectator ions?
Net ionic equation






A REDOX reaction in which a single substance is both oxidized and reduced to form two different products (e.g., H₂O₂ breaking down into H₂O and O₂) is called:
Disproportionation (or Dismutation)






What is the fundamental process that a REDOX titration follows, similar to how an acid-base titration follows the transfer of protons?
Transfer of charge (electrons)






The sum of the oxidation numbers in the polyatomic ion Phosphate (PO₄³⁻) must equal:
-3






When balancing a REDOX reaction in a basic solution, what is used to balance the hydrogen atoms after balancing oxygen with water?
OH⁻ ions






What is the standard oxidation state for a Group 1A metal, like Sodium (Na), when it is in a compound?
+1
