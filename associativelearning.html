Comprehensive MCAT Checklist - Associative Learning and Observational Learning

_________________________________________________________________________________
1. What is the primary difference between associative learning and observational learning?
Associative learning involves making connections between stimuli and responses, while observational learning involves learning by watching others.






_________________________________________________________________________________
2. What is the primary difference between classical conditioning and operant conditioning?
Classical conditioning involves associating two stimuli, while operant conditioning involves associating a behavior with a consequence.






_________________________________________________________________________________
3. Which group would most likely benefit from classical conditioning?
A group of individuals learning to associate a specific sound with a reward.






_________________________________________________________________________________
4. Which best describes the neutral stimulus?
a stimulus that initially does not elicit a response






_________________________________________________________________________________
5. Which best describes the unconditioned stimulus?
a stimulus that naturally elicits a response






_________________________________________________________________________________
6. Which best describes the conditioned stimulus?
a previously neutral stimulus that elicits a response after conditioning






_________________________________________________________________________________
7. Which best describes the conditioned response?
a learned response to a previously neutral stimulus






_________________________________________________________________________________
8. Which of the following is a true statement about classical conditioning?
The neutral stimulus must become a conditioned stimulus, and it is paired with an unconditioned stimulus.
