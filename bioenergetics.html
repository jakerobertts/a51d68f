Bioenergetics & Thermodynamics

Laws of Thermodynamics

  • The first law states that energy cannot be created or destroyed, only converted from one form to another.
  • The second law states that entropy of the universe always increases.

Free Energy

  • Gibbs free energy determines whether a reaction is spontaneous or non-spontaneous.
  • Exergonic reactions release energy (ΔG < 0), while endergonic reactions require energy input (ΔG > 0).
  • ATP hydrolysis provides energy for endergonic reactions through coupled reactions.
  • Le Chatelier's principle states that if a system at equilibrium is disturbed, it will shift to counteract the disturbance.
  • Concentration changes can affect the position of equilibrium, favoring the side with fewer moles of gas.
  • Catabolism refers to the set of metabolic pathways that break down molecules into smaller units and release energy.
  • Anabolic pathways build complex molecules from simpler ones, requiring energy input.

Dive deeper: In catabolism, reactions would primarily be exergonic, releasing energy as they break down complex molecules. Anabolic reactions would be endergonic, requiring energy input to build complex molecules. Hydrolysis reactions are also typically exergonic, as they release energy by breaking down ATP. Synthesis reactions, on the other hand, are generally endergonic, as they require energy input to form complex molecules from simpler ones. Irreversible reactions are those that proceed in one direction and do not reach equilibrium, while reversible reactions can proceed in both directions and reach equilibrium.
