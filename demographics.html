1. The Baby Boomer generation's retirement is creating significant societal changes. Which of the following best describes the primary demographic challenge this presents according to sociological theory?
An age-cohort effect creating both economic opportunities and dependency burden simultaneously






Generational Cohorts and Societal Impact:

Age-Cohort Effects:
Sociologists that study age cohorts find that the older generations, such as the silent and baby boomer generations, still face financial challenges when they retire. Simultaneously, younger generations are opened to new economic opportunities due to the openings of new jobs.

Analysis of options:
A. Incorrect - This describes a period effect, not cohort-specific impact
B. Correct - Captures the dual nature of opportunity and burden from cohort retirement
C. Incorrect - This describes lifecycle effects, ignoring the specific historical context
D. Incorrect - The impact is significant and long-term due to cohort size

MCAT Relevance: Understanding demographic transitions helps explain healthcare system pressures, resource allocation, and social policy challenges in aging societies.

2. Due to medical advancements increasing life expectancy, North America's population aged 65+ is projected to rise from 13.5% to 25% by 2025. Which of the following best explains the primary societal challenge this demographic shift represents?

Dependency Ratio and Population Aging:

Dependency Ratio Definition:
\[ \text{Dependency Ratio} = \frac{\text{Population under 14 + Population over 65}}{\text{Population 15-64}} \times 100 \]

Analysis of options:
A. Incorrect - This is a sustained trend due to medical advances, not temporary
B. Incorrect - Retired individuals typically exit the workforce, reducing productivity
C. Correct - Accurately describes the dependency ratio problem and economic strain
D. Incorrect - This describes benefits of reduced child dependency, but elderly dependency is increasing
