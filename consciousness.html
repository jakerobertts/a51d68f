A. Alpha waves - happens when someone is relaxed, such as daydreaming or meditation.
B. Beta waves - happens when someone is calm, alert, and focused.
C. Theta waves - happens during light sleep or deep relaxation.
D. Delta waves - happens during deep sleep or coma.

Beta waves are associated with active thinking, problem-solving, and focused mental activity. They are typically observed when a person is alert and engaged in cognitive tasks, often accompanied by a certain level of stress or anxiety. Therefore, the description in option B is incorrect because it suggests that beta waves occur when someone is focused but not calm, which is not entirely accurate.

_____________________________________________________________________________ 2. Which of the following may you experience when in the N1 stage of sleep?

A. Seeing or hearing things that aren't there
B. Sleep spindles and K-complexes
C. Difficulty waking up
D. Rapid eye movement (REM) sleep

The N1 stage of sleep, also known as the lightest stage of non-REM sleep, is characterized by a transition from wakefulness to sleep. During this stage, individuals may experience hypnagogic hallucinations, which can include seeing or hearing things that aren't actually present. These hallucinations are often brief and can be quite vivid, making them a common experience during the N1 stage.Therefore, option A is the correct answer.
B. Sleep spindles and K-complexes are patterns that are thought to help with memory consolidation and maintaining sleep stability. They are characteristics of the N2 stage of sleep.
C. Difficulty waking up is more characteristic of the N3 stage of sleep, which is the deepest stage of non-REM sleep. This is due to the synchronization of brain waves during this stage, which also leads to a lack of responsiveness to external stimuli, leading to sleepwalking and sleep talking.
D. Rapid eye movement (REM) sleep is characterized by the presence of vivid dreams and increased brain activity. It typically occurs after the N3 stage and is essential for emotional regulation and memory consolidation. Muscles are paralyzed, preventing the body from acting out dreams.

_____________________________________________________________________________ 3. Which of the following about sleep cycles is FALSE?

A. The first sleep cycle lasts about 90 minutes
B. As the night progresses, REM sleep periods become longer
C. The N3 stage comes before the REM stage
D. The N2 stage comes before the REM stage

The stages of sleep do not present themselves in a linear fashion. Instead, they cycle throughout the night. A typical sleep cycle lasts about 90 minutes and includes the N1, N2, N3, and REM stages. However, after exiting N3, the N2 stages is re-entered before entering REM sleep. Therefore, option C is false because the N3 stage does not come directly before the REM stage; it is followed by the N2 stage first, and N2 occurs twice in a typical sleep cycle.

_____________________________________________________________________________ 4. Circadian rhythms are influenced by which of the following?

A. Melatonin from the pineal gland
B. Light detected by the retina
C. The suprachiasmatic nucleus (SCN) of the hypothalamus
D. All of the above

Circadian rhythms are regulated by a combination of factors, including melatonin production, light exposure, and the suprachiasmatic nucleus (SCN) of the hypothalamus. Melatonin, a hormone produced by the pineal gland, helps signal the body when it's time to sleep. Light detected by the retina influences the SCN, which acts as the body's internal clock, coordinating various physiological processes to align with the 24-hour day-night cycle. Therefore, all of the options listed contribute to the regulation of circadian rhythms, making option D the correct answer.

A. To problem solve
B. To reflect on threats
C. To consolidate and weed out memories
D. To repair neural pathways in the brain

While there are several theories about why we dream, the idea that dreams serve to REFLECT on threats is not a widely accepted or common theory. The evolutionary theory suggests that dreaming may have helped our ancestors practice preparing for threats in a safe environment, but it is not about consciously reflecting on those threats.

_____________________________________________________________________________ 6. Which best describes the activation-synthesis theory of dreaming?

A. Dreams are the brain's attempt to make sense of random neural activity
B. Dreams help consolidate memories and process emotions
C. Dreams are a way to practice responses to threats
D. Dreams are a manifestation of unconscious desires and conflicts

The activation-synthesis theory of dreaming proposes that dreams result from the brain's attempt to make sense of random neural activity that occurs during REM sleep. According to this theory, the brain synthesizes this activity into a coherent narrative, which we experience as dreams. This theory emphasizes the role of physiological processes in dreaming, rather than psychological or emotional factors. Therefore, option A is the correct description of the activation-synthesis theory.

B. The information-processing theory suggests that dreams help consolidate memories and process emotions, not the activation-synthesis theory.
C. The evolutionary theory suggests that dreaming may have helped our ancestors practice preparing for threats in a safe environment, but it is not the activation-synthesis theory.
D. The psychoanalytic theory, proposed by Sigmund Freud, suggests that dreams are a manifestation of unconscious desires and conflicts, which is different from the activation-synthesis theory.

B. Insomnia - excessive daytime sleepiness and sudden muscle weakness

Night terrors are episodes of intense fear, screaming, and flailing while still asleep. They typically occur during the N3 stage of sleep, which is the deepest stage of non-REM sleep. They do NOT occur during REM sleep, which is when most vivid dreaming happens. Night terrors are more common in children and can be quite distressing for both the individual experiencing them and those around them. Therefore, option D is correctly paired with its description.

A. Narcolepsy is characterized by excessive daytime sleepiness and sudden muscle weakness, not difficulty falling or staying asleep.
B. Insomnia is characterized by difficulty falling or staying asleep, not excessive daytime sleepiness and sudden muscle weakness.
C. Obstructive Sleep Apnea is caused by a physical blockage of the airway, not a failure of the brain to send signals to the muscles that control breathing. This is known as a central sleep apnea.

10. Which of the following drug classes is correctly associated with its mechanism of action?

A. Benzodiazepines - enhance the effect of the neurotransmitter GABA at the GABA-A receptor
B. Barbiturates - inhibit GABA-A receptors, leading to increased neuronal excitability
C. Stimulants - increase the reuptake of dopamine, norepinephrine, and serotonin
D. Opiates - bind opioid receptors, which in return releases more opiates

Benzodiazepines are a class of drugs that act as central nervous system depressants. They enhance the effect of the neurotransmitter gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA. at the GABA-A receptor, leading to increased inhibitory effects in the brain. Therefore, option A is correctly associated with its mechanism of action.

B. Barbiturates also enhance the effect of GABA at the GABA-A receptor, leading to increased inhibitory effects, not inhibition of GABA-A receptors.
C. Stimulants increase the release and/or block the reuptake of dopamine, norepinephrine, and serotonin, leading to increased levels of these neurotransmitters in the synaptic cleft. Reuptake would DECREASE their levels in the synaptic cleft.
D. Opiates bind to opioid receptors, leading to increased release of dopamine and other neurotransmitters, not necessarily more opiates.
