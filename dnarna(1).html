DNA Denaturation

  • DNA denaturation is the process of separating the two strands of the double helix, typically induced by heat or chemical treatment.
  • Reannealing occurs when the denatured strands come back together, forming a stable double helix again.
  • Hybridization refers to the binding of complementary DNA or RNA strands from different sources, often used in techniques like blotting and microarray analysis.
