Carboxylic Acids - MCAT Practice Questions

A carboxylic acid functional group is characterized by a carbonyl (C=O) group connected to which other functional group on the same carbon?
Hydroxyl (—OH)






Carboxylic acids are known to have high boiling points primarily due to their ability to form:
Hydrogen bonding (often existing as dimers)






What suffix is used in the IUPAC nomenclature of a carboxylic acid?
-oic acid






Substituents that enhance the acidity of a carboxylic acid are typically:
Electron-withdrawing






What type of chemical reaction removes a carboxyl group and releases carbon dioxide (CO₂)?
Decarboxylation






pKₐ is the pH at which what condition is met for an acid/base species?
Half of the species is deprotonated ([HA] = [A⁻])






Carboxylic acids can be synthesized by the oxidation of 1° alcohols or aldehydes using which of the following?
Potassium permanganate (KMnO₄)






Which strong reducing agent is required to reduce a carboxylic acid to a 1° alcohol?
LiAlH₄ (LAH)






The reaction of a carboxylic acid with an alcohol in the presence of an acid catalyst to form an ester is known as:
Fischer Esterification






In a nucleophilic acyl substitution reaction, a nucleophile attacks the electrophilic carbonyl carbon, forming a tetrahedral intermediate, and then displaces what group?
The leaving group






What reaction describes the ester hydrolysis of triacylglycerols using a strong base like NaOH or KOH?
Saponification






The reagents SOCl₂ (thionyl chloride) and PBr₃ (phosphorus tribromide) are typically used in the synthesis of which carboxylic acid derivative?
Acid halides






An amide is a carboxylic acid derivative in which the hydroxyl (—OH) group has been replaced by a(n):
Amine or ammonia (—NR₂ or —NH₂)






What suffix is used in the naming of an ester?
-oate






A cyclic amide is referred to as a:
Lactam






A cyclic ester is referred to as a:
Lactone






The process of exchanging the R'' group of an ester with the R' group of an alcohol is called:
Transesterification






Which of the following carboxylic acid derivatives is the most reactive toward nucleophilic substitution?
Acid chlorides






Which of the following carboxylic acid derivatives is the least reactive toward nucleophilic substitution?
Amides






A compound formed when a carboxylic acid acts as a nucleophile and attacks a second carboxylic acid is called a(n):
Anhydride






The phenomenon where an uneven distribution of charge across a σ bond occurs due to differences in electronegativity is called:
Induction






Increased ring strain in a molecule typically has what effect on its reactivity?
It makes it more reactive






The term that describes when a reaction cannot proceed or significantly slows because substituents crowd the reactive site is:
Steric hindrance






A high Kₐ value indicates what about an acid?
It is a strong acid






Compared to single bonds, multiple bonds (double or triple) are generally:
Stronger






When a phosphodiester bond is formed (as in DNA backbone formation), what molecule is released?
Pyrophosphate (PPi)






The enol form of a carbonyl compound is an alkene that is also a(n):
Alcohol






The number '2' in an SN2 reaction (Substitution Nucleophilic Bimolecular) refers to the fact that the reaction is:
Bimolecular






A micelle is an organization of molecules that dissolves nonpolar organic substances in its interior and is solvated by water due to what type of exterior shell?
Hydrophilic






A geminal diol is a molecule with two hydroxyl groups attached to:
The same carbon
