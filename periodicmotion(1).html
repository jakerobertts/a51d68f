Periodic Motion

  • The amplitude is the maximum displacement from the equilibrium position.
  • The frequency is the number of cycles per second.
  • The phase is the position of a point in time on a waveform cycle.
  • The wavelength is the distance between successive crests or troughs of a wave.
  • The wave speed is the distance traveled by a wave per unit time, calculated as the product of frequency and wavelength.
  • transverse waves are waves in which the particle displacement is perpendicular to the direction of wave propagation.
  • Longitudinal waves are waves in which the particle displacement is parallel to the direction of wave propagation.
  • Interference is the phenomenon that occurs when two or more waves overlap and combine to form a new wave pattern.
  • Young's double-slit experiment demonstrates the wave nature of light through the observation of interference patterns. The main takeaway is that light behaves as a wave, exhibiting properties such as interference and diffraction.
  • Diffraction is the bending of waves around obstacles and the spreading of waves through openings.
  • Single-slit diffraction is a type of diffraction that occurs when waves pass through a narrow slit, resulting in a pattern of alternating light and dark bands on a screen.
  • Polarization is the orientation of the oscillations of a wave in a particular direction. It can occur in transverse waves, such as light waves.
  • Linear polarization is when the oscillations of a wave are confined to a single plane.
  • Circular polarization is when the oscillations of a wave rotate in a circular motion as the wave propagates.
  • Electromagnetic waves are waves that consist of oscillating electric and magnetic fields, which propagate through space.
  • X-ray diffraction is a technique used to study the structure of materials at the atomic level by observing the patterns produced when X-rays are scattered by a crystal.
  • Oscillating fields are the electric and magnetic fields that vary sinusoidally in time and space in electromagnetic waves.
  • The electromagnetic spectrum is the range of all frequencies of electromagnetic radiation, from radio waves to gamma rays.
  • Photon energy is the energy carried by a single photon, which is directly proportional to the frequency of the electromagnetic wave.
