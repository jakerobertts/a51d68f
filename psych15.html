Psychology - Social Influence and Deviance - MCAT Practice Questions

A newly hired employee begins wearing a specific uniform color only because they observe all their successful colleagues doing so, believing this is the correct way to dress for success. This is an example of:
Normative Social Influence






A person publicly states they agree with a group's opinion on a controversial topic, even though they privately disagree, simply to avoid confrontation and ensure they are accepted. This action is motivated by:
Normative Social Influence






Which level of Conformity is the lowest, involving temporary or superficial change to public behavior without any change to private beliefs?
Compliance






A person truly integrates a religious group's values and beliefs into their personal ethical system, demonstrating the deepest level of conformity known as:
Internalization






Labeling Theory proposes that deviance is primarily defined by:
Society's reaction to the act






A student occasionally copies homework answers from a friend but is not caught and does not internalize the identity of a "cheater." This isolated, unpunished act is an example of:
Primary Deviance






A person who was involved in Primary Deviance is arrested, publicly labeled a "criminal," and subsequently finds it nearly impossible to get a job, leading them to commit more crimes. This subsequent, more serious behavior is known as:
Secondary Deviance






A city-wide panic erupts as people share unfounded rumors via social media about contaminated drinking water, causing a run on bottled water stores. This collective behavior is best described as:
Mass Hysteria






A crowd, feeling marginalized and angered by a local court decision, expresses its frustration through property destruction and violent clashes. This is a classic example of a:
Riot






The transition from Primary Deviance to Secondary Deviance is primarily driven by:
The societal reaction and stigmatizing label
