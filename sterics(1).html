Stereochemistry

  • Types of stereoisomers:
  • Enantiomers: Non-superimposable mirror images.
    • Enantiomers have identical physical properties except for their interaction with plane-polarized light.
    • An object is chiral if it is not superimposable on its mirror image.
    • Chirality centers (stereocenters) are typically carbon atoms bonded to four different groups.
    • They are prioritized based on their atomic number.
    • R/S configuration is assigned based on the arrangement of groups around the chirality center.
  • Diastereomers: Stereoisomers that are not mirror images.
    • Diastereomers have different physical properties and reactivities.
  • Geometric isomers: Stereoisomers with different spatial arrangements around a double bond or ring structure.
  • E/Z nomenclature is used to describe the geometry of double bonds, based on the priority of substituents attached to the double-bonded carbons.
