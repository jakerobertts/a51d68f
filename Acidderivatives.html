1. Which best describes the reactivity of acyl halides?A polar molecule with a dipole moment with higher boiling points than alkanes

a. More reactive than carboxylic acids
b. More reactive than esters
c. More reactive than amides
d. All of the above

Acyl halides are the most reactive out of all carboxylic acids and its derivatives due to the presence of a good leaving group (the halide ion) and the strong electrophilicity of the carbonyl carbon. This makes them highly susceptible to nucleophilic attack, leading to their increased reactivity. Since a halogen like chlorine is a good leaving group because it can be on its own, it makes acyl chlorides particularly reactive.

Acid anhydrides are typically formed through the condensation of two carboxylic acid molecules. In this reaction, a molecule of water is eliminated as the two carboxylic acids combine, resulting in the formation of an acid anhydride.This process is often facilitated by heating or using a dehydrating agent to drive off the water and shift the equilibrium toward the formation of the anhydride.However, since acyl chlorides are highly reactive, they can also react with carboxylic acids to form acid anhydrides.
