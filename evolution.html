Evolution & Genetics

  • Natural selection is the process by which organisms with favorable traits survive and reproduce more successfully.
  • Gene flow is the transfer of genetic material between separate populations.
  • Mutation introduces new genetic variation into populations.

    • Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium

    • The Hardy Weinberg principle describes allele frequencies in non-evolving populations.
    • Conditions include no mutations, no selection, and random mating.
    • Speciation is the process by which new species arise.
    • Polymorphism is the occurrence of different forms or alleles in a population.
    • Adaption is the process by which a species becomes better suited to its environment.
    • Inbreeding is the mating of closely related individuals, which can increase the risk of genetic disorders.
    • The Bottleneck effect is a sharp reduction in the size of a population due to environmental events or human activities.
    • The Founder effect occurs when a small group establishes a new population, leading to reduced genetic variation.
    • Evolutionary time is measured by gradual changes in the genome.
