_____________________________________________________________________________ 1. A deficiency in a hormone will most likely lead to a diminished binding of:

A. The hormone to a general receptor in one type of tissue
B. The hormone to a general receptor in all types of tissue
C. The hormone to its specific receptor in various tissues
D. The hormone to its specific receptor in one type of tissue

A hormone deficiency typically results in reduced binding of the hormone to its specific receptor in various tissues. Hormones often have specific receptors in different tissues, and a deficiency can affect multiple tissues that rely on that hormone for regulation.

A. This is incorrect. A deficiency would affect binding in multiple tissues, not just one.Also, the hormone receptor is specific, not general.
B. This is incorrect. Hormones bind to specific receptors, not general ones, and a deficiency would affect multiple tissues.
D. This is incorrect. A deficiency would affect binding in multiple tissues, not just one.

_____________________________________________________________________________ 2. A student stated a cell with endocrine function would secrete a substance to exert its effect on itself. Which of the following revisions would be most appropriate? The cell would secrete its substance into the bloodstream instead of on itself.

A. The cell would secrete its substance into the bloodstream instead of on itself.
B. The cell would secrete its substance into the interstitial fluid instead of on itself.
C. The cell would secrete its substance into neighboring gap junctions instead of on itself.
D. The cell would secrete its substance into the digestive tract instead of on itself.

A cell with endocrine function secretes hormones into the bloodstream to exert effects on distant target cells. Endocrine cells release hormones that travel through the bloodstream to reach target cells located in different parts of the body. These substances are known as hormones. This is how the endocrine system modulates various physiological processes.

B. This is incorrect. This effect on neighboring cells is characteristic of paracrine signaling, not endocrine signaling.
C. This is incorrect. Gap junctions facilitate direct cell-to-cell communication, which is not the mechanism of endocrine signaling.
D. This is incorrect. The digestive tract is not involved in endocrine signaling; hormones are released into the bloodstream.

_____________________________________________________________________________ 3. A patient presents with the following findings:

Decreased cortisol and aldosterone
Increased ACTH
Elevated CRH
Normal epinephrine/norepinephrine levels
No change in FSH, LH, TSH, prolactin, growth hormone, or endorphins

Which of the following areas of the endocrine system is mostly affected? Adrenal Cortex

A. Anterior Pituitary
B. Ovaries
C. Thyroid
D. Adrenal Cortex

The adrenal cortex is most likely affected in this patient. The symptoms and lab findings are consistent with Addison's disease, which is characterized by adrenal insufficiency. Higher levels of ACH and CRH indicate a lack of negative feedback from cortisol, which is produced by the adrenal cortex.

A. This is incorrect. The anterior pituitary produces ACTH, but a higher release is likely due to low cortisol levels secreted by the adrenal cortex because of the lack of negative feedback.
B. This is incorrect. The ovaries are involved in the production of follicles and are influenced by FSH and LH. These levels are normal in this patient.
C. This is incorrect. TSH is released by the anterior pituitary and stimulates the thyroid gland, but thyroid hormone levels are not affected in this patient.



4. What symptoms would you LEAST expect in a patient with these findings? Decreased lactation (breast milk production)

A. Fatigue and muscle weakness
B. Decreased lactation
C. Weight loss and low blood pressure
D. Elevated activity of the HPA axis

Decreased lactation (breast milk production) is the symptom you would least expect in a patient with adrenal insufficiency. Lactation is primarily regulated by prolactin, which is not affected in this case.The other symptoms listed are more directly related to the effects of low cortisol and aldosterone levels seen in adrenal insufficiency.

A. This is incorrect. Fatigue and muscle weakness are common symptoms of adrenal insufficiency due to low cortisol levels.
C. This is incorrect. Weight loss and low blood pressure are typical symptoms of adrenal insufficiency due to decreased aldosterone and cortisol.
D. This is incorrect. Elevated activity of the HPA axis is expected as the body attempts to compensate for low cortisol levels by increasing CRH and ACTH production.



_____________________________________________________________________________ 5. Which of the following would NOT be a cause of release of hormones from the anterior pituitary? Depolarization of axon terminals and exocytosis

A. Hormones from the hypothalamus binding to receptors
B. Negative feedback from target organs
C. An influx of neurohormones from the hypothalamus
D. Exocytosis of hypothalamic hormones by depolarization

Depolarization of axon terminals and exocytosis is NOT a cause of hormone release from the anterior pituitary. The anterior pituitary releases hormones in response to signals from the hypothalamus, not through direct neural stimulation. The latter is characteristic of the posterior pituitary, where hormones are released directly into the bloodstream in response to neural signals after being storeD. Therefore, option D is the correct answer.



_____________________________________________________________________________ 6. Which of the following would you expect to see in the secretion of a tyrosine-derived hormone? Triggering of a G-Protein Coupled Receptor

A. Triggering of a G-Protein Coupled Receptor
B. Direct entry into the cell and binding to intracellular receptors
C. Binding to receptor tyrosine kinases on the cell surface
D. Activation of ion channels on the cell membrane

Tyrosine-derived hormones, such as epinephrine and norepinephrine, often trigger G-Protein Coupled Receptors (GPCRs) on the cell surface. These hormones bind to GPCRs, leading to the activation of intracellular signaling pathways that mediate their effects. This secondary messenger system often uses cyclic AMP (cAMP) as a key signaling molecule.

B. This is incorrect. Tyrosine-derived hormones typically do not enter the cell directly; they bind to surface receptors.
C. This is incorrect. While some hormones may interact with receptor tyrosine kinases, tyrosine-derived hormones primarily use GPCRs.
D. This is incorrect. Activation of ion channels is not the primary mechanism for tyrosine-derived hormones.



_____________________________________________________________________________ 7. Which of the following hormones can you MOST likely expect to be bound to a water-soluble carrier protein in the bloodstream? Thyroid Hormone (T3 and T4)

A. A hormone that relies on a G-Protein Coupled Receptor (GPCR)
B. A hormone that is primarily water-soluble
C. A hormone derived from cholesterol
D. A hormone that is dissolved in the plasma

Hormones derived from cholesterol, such as thyroid hormones (T3 and T4), are typically bound to carrier proteins in the bloodstream. The reason for this is that cholesterol-derived hormones are lipid-soluble and do not dissolve well in the aqueous environment of the blood. Carrier proteins help transport these hormones to their target tissues.

A. This is incorrect. Hormones that rely on GPCRs are often water-soluble and do not require carrier proteins.
B. This is incorrect. Hormones that are primarily water-soluble do not typically require carrier proteins.
D. This is incorrect. Hormones that are dissolved in the plasma are usually water-soluble and do not require carrier proteins.



_____________________________________________________________________________ 8. Which of the following regulations would most likely result in a decrease in blood pressure? Increased potassium levels?

A. Increased conversion of Angiotensin I to Angiotensin II by ACE
B. Increased antidiuretic hormone (ADH) secretion
C. Retention of fluid
D. Increased potassium levels

Increased potassium levels would most likely result in a decrease in blood pressure. Elevated potassium levels can lead to vasodilation, which decreases vascular resistance and subsequently lowers blood pressure. Additionally, high potassium levels can inhibit the release of aldosterone, a hormone that promotes sodium and water retention, which can also contribute to lower blood pressure.

A. This is incorrect. Increased conversion of Angiotensin I to Angiotensin II by ACE leads to vasoconstriction and an increase in blood pressure.
B. This is incorrect. Increased antidiuretic hormone (ADH) secretion promotes water retention, which increases blood volume and raises blood pressure.
C. This is incorrect. Retention of fluid increases blood volume, which raises blood pressure.



_____________________________________________________________________________ 9. Negative feedback systems are crucial to maintaining homeostasis in the endocrine system because: They prevent excessive hormone production and maintain balance

A. They amplify the effects of hormones to ensure a strong response
B. They prevent excessive hormone production
C. They allow hormones to act on multiple target tissues simultaneously
D. They facilitate the rapid degradation of hormones in the bloodstream

Negative feedback systems are crucial in the endocrine system because they prevent excessive hormone production and help maintain balance. When hormone levels rise above a certain threshold, negative feedback mechanisms signal the endocrine glands to reduce hormone secretion. This helps maintain homeostasis by ensuring that hormone levels remain within a normal range, preventing overproduction that could lead to adverse effects.

A. This is incorrect. Amplifying hormone effects is characteristic of positive feedback, not negative feedback.
C. This is incorrect. While hormones can act on multiple tissues, this is not the primary function of negative feedback.
D. This is incorrect. Hormone degradation is not the main role of negative feedback; it focuses on regulating hormone production.



_____________________________________________________________________________ 10. Which enzyme triggers the phosphorylation cascade in ion channels? Protein Kinase A

A. Protein Kinase A
B. Adenylyl Cyclase
C. Phospholipase C
D. Tyrosine Kinase

Protein Kinase A (PKA. is the enzyme that triggers the phosphorylation cascade in ion channels. PKA is activated by cyclic AMP (cAMP), which is produced by the enzyme adenylyl cyclase in response to the activation of G-Protein Coupled Receptors (GPCRs). Once activated, PKA phosphorylates specific target proteins, including ion channels, leading to changes in their activity and function.

B. This is incorrect. Adenylyl Cyclase is responsible for converting ATP to cAMP, but it does not directly trigger the phosphorylation cascade.
C. This is incorrect. Phospholipase C is involved in a different signaling pathway that generates IP3 and DAG, not in the phosphorylation cascade of ion channels.
D. This is incorrect. Tyrosine Kinase is involved in receptor tyrosine kinase signaling, not in the phosphorylation cascade triggered by PKA.
