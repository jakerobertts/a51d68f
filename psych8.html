Psychology - Personality Theories - MCAT Practice Questions

Which perspective of personality states that personality results from unconscious urges and desires?
Psychoanalytic perspective






Freud's theory states that personality is formed through conflicts among which three fundamental structures of the human mind?
Id, superego, and ego






In Freud's theory of personality, which structure is in charge of our base urges of survival and reproduction?
Id






Which Freudian structure is described as the idealist and perfectionist?
Superego






The structure that acts as the mediator between the id, the superego, and the conscious mind is the:
Ego






Jung's theory of collective unconscious states that personality is influenced by:
Archetypes






According to the theories of Adler and Horney, what primarily motivates our unconscious?
Social urges






Which perspective emphasizes the internal feelings of healthy individuals as they strive for happiness and self-realization?
Humanistic perspective






Carl Rogers' concept involving showing complete support and acceptance of a person no matter what that person says or does is known as:
Unconditional positive regard






Which theory is the belief that an individual's personality can be quantified into a few unique categories (e.g., Type A and Type B)?
Type theory






Hans Eysenck's theory of three dimensions of personality (the PEN model) includes Psychoticism, Neuroticism, and:
Extraversion






The Big 5 personality traits are known by the mnemonic OCEAN. What does the 'C' stand for?
Conscientiousness






According to Gordon Allport's trait theory, which traits are the major characteristics of personality?
Central traits






Which perspective of personality states that individuals react with their environment in a cycle called reciprocal determinism?
Social cognitive perspective






The perspective that states our personality develops as a result of operant conditioning is the:
Behaviorist perspective
