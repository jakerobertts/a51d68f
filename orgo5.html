Spectroscopy - MCAT Practice Questions

Infrared spectroscopy measures the absorption of IR light, which causes what effect in a molecule?
Molecular vibration (stretching, bending, etc.)






The characteristic IR absorption frequency for a C=O (carbonyl) bond is approximately:
1750 cm⁻¹






The IR frequency range of 3000 - 3300 cm⁻¹ is characteristic of which bond, often appearing as a broad signal?
O—H






A compound containing a nitrile (C≡N) bond would likely show an IR peak in which frequency range?
1900 - 2200 cm⁻¹






Ultraviolet spectroscopy measures the absorption of UV light, which causes what specific molecular event?
Movement of electrons between molecular orbitals






Which technique measures the alignment of nuclear spin with an applied magnetic field to determine molecular structure?
NMR spectroscopy






What compound is used for calibration in NMR spectroscopy, defining the chemical shift of 0 ppm?
Tetramethylsilane (TMS)






In NMR spectroscopy, the area under each peak (signal), which corresponds to the number of protons in that signal, is known as the:
Integration






A proton that is deshielded in an NMR spectrum appears in what region?
Downfield (left)






The phenomenon where hydrogens on adjacent atoms interfere with each other's magnetic environment, causing peaks to split, is known as:
Spin-spin coupling (splitting)






According to the n+1 rule for NMR multiplicity, what does the variable 'n' represent?
The number of protons on the adjacent (adjoining) carbon atom(s)






A signal in the NMR range of 6.5 - 8.0 ppm indicates the presence of hydrogens from which type of group?
Aryl






The most downfield signals in ¹H NMR (approximately 11.0 - 12.0 ppm) are characteristic of the hydrogens from which functional group?
Carboxylic acid






The NMR range of 9.7 - 10.0 ppm is characteristic of which hydrogen type?
Aldehyde group






Deshielding in NMR spectroscopy is caused by the removal of electron density and results in a(n):
Increase in the ppm value






A mass spectrometer determines the structure of an ionized molecule by deflecting the ions through a magnetic field based on the ion's:
Mass-to-charge ratio (m/z)






In a mass spectrum, the peak corresponding to the heaviest ion (greatest m/z value) is most likely the:
Molecular ion peak (M)






The largest peak in a mass spectrum, corresponding to the most abundant ion, is known as the:
Base peak






The small M+1 peak, located one m/z unit to the right of the molecular ion peak, is primarily due to the natural abundance of which isotope?
¹³C






A mass spectrum shows an M peak and an M+2 peak that are roughly equal in height (a 1:1 ratio). This pattern is characteristic of a molecule containing which element?
Bromine
