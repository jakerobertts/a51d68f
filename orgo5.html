Acid Derivatives - MCAT Practice Questions

What molecule is released when a phosphodiester bond is formed?
Pyrophosphate (PPi)






_________________________________________________________________________________

The pyrophosphate (PPi) released during a phosphodiester bond formation can be hydrolyzed into what two compounds?
Two inorganic phosphates (Pi)






________________________________________________________________________________

Cyclic amides are commonly called what?
Lactams






________________________________________________________________________________

Cyclic esters are commonly called what?
Lactones






________________________________________________________________________________

What two functional groups characterize the enol form of a molecule like acetone?
An alcohol (—OH) and an alkene (C=C)






________________________________________________________________________________

How does the keto form differ structurally from the enol form?
The keto form contains a regular ketone or aldehyde (C=O) group






________________________________________________________________________________

A high Kₐ (acid dissociation constant) value indicates what about the strength of an acid?
It is a strong acid






________________________________________________________________________________

The pKₐ value is defined as the pH at which the concentration of the acid ([HA]) is equal to what?
The concentration of the conjugate base ([A⁻]), so [HA] = [A⁻]






________________________________________________________________________________

If an acid has a very low Kₐ, what can be said about its corresponding pKₐ value?
It will have a high pKₐ






________________________________________________________________________________

In general, are multiple bonds (double or triple) stronger or weaker than single bonds?
Stronger






________________________________________________________________________________

Which bond (C—C single, C=C double, or C≡C triple) has the highest overall bond strength?
The C≡C triple bond






________________________________________________________________________________

The number '2' in an SN2 reaction refers to the fact that the reaction is what?
Bimolecular (two species in the rate-determining step)






________________________________________________________________________________

Given the rate law Rate = k[substrate]¹[nucleophile]¹, what is the overall reaction order for the SN2 reaction?
Second-order (1 + 1 = 2)






________________________________________________________________________________

If the pH is exactly equal to the pKₐ of a weak acid, what is the numerical ratio of the conjugate base to the weak acid ([A⁻]/[HA])?
1 (or 1:1)






________________________________________________________________________________

If Acid A has a pKₐ of 2.0 and Acid B has a pKₐ of 5.0, which acid is stronger?
Acid A (Lower pKₐ = Stronger Acid)
