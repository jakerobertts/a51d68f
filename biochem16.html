Biochemistry - Pentose Phosphate Pathway & Urea Cycle - MCAT Practice Questions

What is the starting material for the Pentose Phosphate Pathway (PPP)?
Glucose 6-phosphate






What are the two main products of the Pentose Phosphate Pathway?
NADPH and pentose sugars






NADPH produced by the PPP is primarily used for which of the following processes?
Fatty acid synthesis, steroid synthesis, and antioxidant defense






What type of sugar is ribose 5-phosphate?
Pentose sugar (5-carbon sugar)






Ribose 5-phosphate is essential for the synthesis of which biomolecules?
Nucleotides (DNA, RNA) and coenzymes






What is the primary function of the Urea Cycle?
Convert toxic ammonia to less toxic urea






Where does the Urea Cycle take place?
Cytosol and mitochondrial matrix of the liver






Ammonia that enters the Urea Cycle is primarily a byproduct of which metabolic process?
Amino acid metabolism






The Urea Cycle is metabolically connected to the citric acid cycle through which two compounds?
Oxaloacetate and fumarate






How does the PPP convert NADP⁺ to NADPH for use in anabolic pathways?
Through oxidation-reduction reactions during glucose 6-phosphate metabolism
