Biology - The Endocrine System - Other Hormones - MCAT Practice Questions

Which hormone is responsible for regulating blood calcium levels by promoting calcium reabsorption in the kidneys?
Parathyroid hormone (PTH)






Calcitonin is produced by which cells and has what primary function?
C cells of the thyroid; lowers blood calcium






Which hormone is produced by the pineal gland and regulates circadian rhythms?
Melatonin






Erythropoietin (EPO) is primarily produced by which organ and stimulates what process?
Kidneys; red blood cell production






Which pancreatic hormone opposes the action of insulin by raising blood glucose levels?
Glucagon






Leptin is produced by which tissue and has what primary function in metabolism?
Adipose tissue; suppresses appetite






Ghrelin is known as the "hunger hormone" and is primarily produced by which organ?
Stomach






Which hormone is produced by the heart and helps regulate blood pressure and fluid balance?
Atrial natriuretic peptide (ANP)






Gastrin is produced by G cells in the stomach and primarily stimulates the secretion of:
Hydrochloric acid






Which hormone produced by the small intestine stimulates the release of digestive enzymes from the pancreas?
Cholecystokinin (CCK)






Secretin is released in response to acidic chyme entering the duodenum and primarily stimulates:
Bicarbonate secretion from the pancreas






Which thyroid hormone is more biologically active and has a shorter half-life?
T3 (triiodothyronine)






Cortisol is classified as which type of hormone and is produced in which zone of the adrenal cortex?
Glucocorticoid; zona fasciculata






Which hormone is produced by the corpus luteum and helps maintain pregnancy in early stages?
Progesterone






Testosterone is primarily produced by which cells in the testes?
Leydig cells






Which hormone produced by the placenta is detected in pregnancy tests?
hCG (human chorionic gonadotropin)






Inhibin is produced by which cells and has what effect on FSH secretion?
Sertoli/granulosa cells; inhibits FSH






Which hormone stimulates milk production in mammary glands?
Prolactin






Vitamin D3 (calcitriol) functions as a hormone and primarily regulates the absorption of which minerals?
Calcium and phosphate






Which enzyme converts angiotensinogen to angiotensin I in the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system?
Renin
