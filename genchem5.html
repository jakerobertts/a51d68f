General Chemistry - Solutions - MCAT Practice Questions

A chemist is preparing a concentrated solution of HCl. The liquid component that is capable of dissolving the HCl gas is functionally classified as the:
Solvent






The process by which NaCl solid breaks apart into its constituent ions upon being added to water is referred to as solvation or:
Dissolution






The maximum concentration of PbCl₂ that can exist in an aqueous solution at 25°C before precipitation begins is best quantified by the substance's:
Solubility






In a 1.0 L saturated solution containing 0.005 mol of dissolved AgCl, the 0.005 mol/L value specifically represents the what of AgCl?
Molar solubility






What type of ionic species consists of a central metal ion bonded to multiple ligands, such as Ag(NH₃)₂⁺?
Complex ion






Lipids, which are nonpolar molecules, primarily interact with water through minimal attractive forces, leading to their common classification as:
Hydrophobic






A molecule with distinct positive and negative poles will readily form hydrogen bonds with water and is therefore classified as:
Hydrophilic






A 1.0 M solution of H₂SO₄ can release two protons per molecule. This fact implies that the H₂SO₄ solution has what of 2.0 N?
Normality






According to general solubility rules, a salt containing the NH₄⁺ cation and the NO₃⁻ anion will be:
Always soluble






A compound forms between Hg₂²⁺ and the I⁻ ion. Based on solubility rules, the resulting product is expected to be:
Insoluble, as Hg₂²⁺ is an exception for halides






Which of the following ions forms a soluble salt when paired with the SO₄²⁻ anion?
K⁺






PO₄³⁻ and CO₃²⁻ are generally insoluble except when paired with:
Na⁺, K⁺, and NH₄⁺






What thermodynamic constant represents the equilibrium state for a heterogeneous reaction in which a sparingly soluble ionic solid is dissolving into an aqueous solution?
Ksp






For the sparingly soluble compound Ca(OH)₂, which dissociates as Ca(OH)₂(s) ⇌ Ca²⁺(aq) + 2OH⁻(aq), the Ksp expression is:
[Ca²⁺][OH⁻]²






If the value of which parameter, calculated using non-equilibrium ion concentrations, is found to be greater than the solubility product constant, precipitation will occur?
Reaction quotient (Qsp)






A chemist mixes two solutions, and finds that the Qsp is less than the Ksp for the potential precipitate. This means the resulting solution is:
Unsaturated and no precipitate will form






If a solution is at a point where Qsp = Ksp, the solution is characterized as:
Saturated and at equilibrium






Adding a source of Cl⁻ (e.g., NaCl) to a saturated solution of AgCl will cause the solubility of the AgCl to:
Decrease due to the common ion effect






EDTA is a molecule that bonds to a central metal ion at multiple points, making it a classic example of what type of ligand?
Chelate






Boiling point elevation, freezing point depression, and osmotic pressure are all examples of what properties, which depend only on the number of particles in solution?
Colligative






The presence of 1.0 mol of nonvolatile NaCl in a solvent will cause a greater change in the boiling point of the solvent compared to 1.0 mol of the nonvolatile solute glucose due to the difference in:
Osmolarity (number of particles)






Raoult's law provides a method for calculating what above an ideal solution?
Vapor pressure






Adding a nonvolatile solute to water causes what in the partial pressure of the water vapor above the liquid solution?
Reduction (lowering)






A 1.0 M NaCl solution has a higher value for what than a 1.0 M C₁₂H₂₂O₁₁ (sucrose) solution?
Osmolarity






Based on solubility rules, a salt containing the CrO₄⁻ anion is generally insoluble except when paired with four specific cations: Na⁺, K⁺, NH₄⁺, and:
Mg²⁺
