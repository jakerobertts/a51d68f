Regulation

  • Allosteric regulation involves the binding of a molecule to a site other than the active site, causing a conformational change that affects enzyme activity. This type of regulation can either enhance or inhibit enzyme activity, depending on the nature of the effector molecule.
  • Covalent modification involves the addition or removal of a chemical group (e.g., phosphate) to/from the enzyme, leading to changes in activity.
  • Feedback inhibition is a regulatory mechanism in which the end product of a metabolic pathway inhibits an upstream process, preventing the overproduction of the product.
  • A zymogen is an inactive precursor of an enzyme that requires a biochemical change (e.g., cleavage) to become active.
