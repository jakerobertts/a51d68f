Feeder Pathways

  • Feeder pathways are metabolic routes that provide substrates for central metabolic pathways like glycolysis and the citric acid cycle. They include pathways such as glycogenolysis, and the pentose phosphate pathway.

    • Gluconeogenesis

    • Gluconeogenesis is the synthesis of glucose from non-carbohydrate precursors, primarily occurring in the liver and kidneys.
    • It involves reversing glycolysis, with some unique steps to bypass irreversible reactions.
    • The first step is catalyzed by pyruvate carboxylase, converting pyruvate to oxaloacetate in the mitochondria.
    • Oxaloacetate is then converted to phosphoenolpyruvate by PEP carboxykinase.
    • The rate limiting step is catalyzed by fructose-1,6-bisphosphatase.
