Apoptosis & Cell Death

Programmed Cell Death

  • Apoptosis is a controlled process of cell death that occurs during development and tissue homeostasis.
  • Necrosis is uncontrolled cell death resulting from injury or disease, causing inflammation.
  • Autophagy is the process by which cells break down and recycle their own components.
  • Anoikis is apoptosis triggered by loss of cell attachment to the extracellular matrix.

Apoptotic Pathways

  • The intrinsic pathway is triggered by internal cellular stress and involves mitochondria.
  • The extrinsic pathway is initiated by external signals through death receptors on the cell surface.
  • Death receptors are cell surface proteins that bind to death ligands to initiate apoptosis.
  • Cytochrome c is released from mitochondria to activate caspases in the intrinsic pathway.

Apoptotic Machinery

  • Caspases are proteases that cleave cellular proteins to execute apoptosis.
  • Initiator caspases are activated first and trigger the apoptotic cascade.
  • Executioner caspases cleave cellular substrates to dismantle the cell.
  • Apoptosome is a protein complex that activates initiator caspases in the intrinsic pathway.

Bcl-2 Family Proteins

  • Bcl-2 is an anti-apoptotic protein that prevents mitochondrial permeabilization.
  • Bax is a pro-apoptotic protein that promotes mitochondrial membrane permeabilization.
  • BH3-only proteins are sensors that detect cellular stress and initiate apoptosis.
  • MOMP is the permeabilization of mitochondrial outer membrane that releases apoptotic factors.

Apoptotic Features

  • DNA fragmentation is the systematic breakdown of chromosomal DNA during apoptosis.
  • Chromatin condensation is the compaction of nuclear material into dense masses.
  • Membrane blebbing is the formation of bubble-like protrusions from the cell surface.
  • Apoptotic bodies are membrane-bound fragments containing cellular components.

Regulation and Significance

  • p53 can induce apoptosis in response to severe DNA damage.
  • Phagocytosis is the process by which apoptotic cells are cleared by macrophages.
  • Eat-me signals are molecules displayed by apoptotic cells to attract phagocytes.
  • Cancer often involves defects in apoptotic pathways, allowing abnormal cells to survive.
