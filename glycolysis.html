Glycolysis & Pyruvate Processing

Overview

  • Glycolysis is the metabolic pathway that breaks down glucose into pyruvate, producing ATP and NADH.
  • Glycolysis occurs in the cytoplasm

Key Steps

  • Hexokinase phosphorylates glucose to glucose-6-phosphate using ATP.
  • Phosphofructokinase (PFK) is the rate-limiting enzyme and key regulatory step by catalyzing the conversion of fructose-6-phosphate to fructose-1,6-bisphosphate.
  • Pyruvate kinase catalyzes the final step, producing pyruvate and ATP.

Energy Yield

  • Net yield is 2 ATP and 2 NADH per glucose molecule.
  • Fermentation can occur if oxygen is not available, converting pyruvate to lactate or ethanol, regenerating NAD⁺.

Pyruvate Processing

  • Pyruvate is transported into the mitochondria of the cell, where it is converted to acetyl-CoA by the pyruvate dehydrogenase complex.
  • This process produces NADH and releases CO₂.
