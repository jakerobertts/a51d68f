DNA in Prokaryotes VS Eukaryotes

  • The Jacob-Monad operon model describes the regulation of gene expression in prokaryotes, where genes are organized into operons that can be turned on or off as a unit.
  • The operon concept includes a promoter, an operator, and structural genes. The promoter is where RNA polymerase binds to initiate transcription, the operator is a regulatory region that can block RNA polymerase binding, and the structural genes are the actual genes being transcribed.
  • Genes are repressed in bacteria by repressors, which bind to the operator and prevent RNA polymerase from transcribing the genes.
  • Positive control in prokaryotes involves activators, which bind to the promoter or enhancer regions to facilitate RNA polymerase binding and transcription initiation.
  • In eukaryotes, DNA is regulated through a complex interplay of transcription factors, enhancers, and silencers, allowing for precise control of gene expression in response to developmental and environmental cues.
