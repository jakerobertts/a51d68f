Types of Feedback Regulation

  • Negative feedback is a regulatory mechanism where the end product of a metabolic pathway inhibits an upstream process, preventing overproduction of the product. An example is the regulation of blood glucose levels, where high glucose levels stimulate insulin release, which in turn lowers blood glucose levels.
  • Positive feedback is a regulatory mechanism that amplifies the production of a product. This occurs in certain biological processes, such as reproduction, where the presence of a hormone stimulates the production of more hormones.
