Electrophoresis

  • Electrophoresis is a technique used to separate charged particles in a gel or solution by applying an electric field.
  • The particles migrate towards the electrode of opposite charge, with the rate of migration depending on their size and charge
  • Agarose gel electrophoresis is commonly used for separating DNA fragments based on size.
  • Polyacrylamide gel electrophoresis is used for separating proteins and smaller DNA fragments.

Dive deeper: In agarose gel electrophoresis, DNA fragments are separated based on size, with smaller fragments migrating faster through the gel matrix. In polyacrylamide gel electrophoresis, proteins are separated based on their size and charge, often using denaturing conditions to ensure consistent migration. Migration distance is proportional to charge and the electric field, and is inversely proportional to the friction of the gel. Native page electrophoresis separates proteins based on their native charge and size, while SDS-PAGE denatures proteins and separates them based on size alone.
