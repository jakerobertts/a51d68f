Alleles & Genes

  • A gene is a segment of DNA that codes for a specific protein or functional RNA.
  • An allele is a variant form of a gene that can result in different traits or phenotypes.
  • For every gene in each human, there are two alleles, leading to genetic variation within a population.
  • Alleles can be dominant or recessive. A dominant allele masks the effect of a recessive allele when both are present in an individual.
  • An individual with two identical alleles for a trait is said to be homozygous, while an individual with two different alleles is heterozygous.
  • A genotype is the genetic makeup of an individual, while a phenotype is the observable expression of that genotype.
  • The wild-type allele is the most common form of a gene in a population, while mutant alleles are less common and can arise from mutations.
  • A locus is the specific location of a gene on a chromosome, and alleles at a locus can vary in sequence, leading to different traits.
  • Alleles can undergo incomplete dominance or codominance, where both alleles contribute to the phenotype in different ways.
  • Leakage is the transfer of alleles between species, which can occur through hybridization or horizontal gene transfer.
  • Penetrance refers to the proportion of individuals with a specific genotype that express the expected phenotype, while expressivity refers to the degree to which a genotype is expressed in an individual.
  • Genetic Drift is the change in allele frequencies in a population due to random sampling events, which can lead to the loss of genetic variation over time.
  • Founder Effect occurs when a small group of individuals establishes a new population, leading to reduced genetic variation compared to the original population.
  • Gene pool refers to the total collection of alleles in a population, which can change over time due to factors like natural selection, mutation, and genetic drift.

    • A generation of 1:1 ratio of blue to red flowers would demonstrate codominance. A homozygous red and homozygous white flower produce pink flowers, which would demonstrate incomplete dominance. Individuals with the same allele for skin color may exhibit different shades due to environmental factors, which demonstrates expressivity. Some may not display a shade at all, regardless of the allele, representing penetrance.
