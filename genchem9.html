General Chemistry - Electrochemistry - MCAT Practice Questions

Which of the following statements correctly compares a galvanic cell to an electrolytic cell?
A galvanic cell converts chemical energy into electrical energy, while an electrolytic cell does the reverse






In any electrochemical cell, which statement is true about the electrodes?
The anode is the site of oxidation and attracts anions






In an operating electrochemical cell, what is the path of electron flow through the external circuit?
From the anode to the cathode






A REDOX reaction has a standard cell potential (E°cell) of +1.10 V. Which conclusion about the reaction is thermodynamically sound?
The reaction is spontaneous, and the ΔG° is negative






Given the following standard reduction potentials:
Zn²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Zn(s); E° = -0.76 V
Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu(s); E° = +0.34 V
What is the standard EMF (E°cell) for the galvanic cell constructed from these two half-cells?
+1.10 V






A galvanic cell involves the transfer of 2 moles of electrons (n=2) and has a standard cell potential (E°cell) of +0.80 V. What is the standard Gibbs free energy change (ΔG°) for this reaction? (ΔG° = -nFE°cell; F ≈ 96,500 J/(V·mol))
-154,400 J/mol






A table of standard reduction potentials shows that substance P has E° = +2.87 V and substance Q has E° = -2.71 V. Which statement is correct?
The oxidized form of P is the strongest oxidizing agent






In an electrolytic concentration cell with Fe electrodes, the half-cell with the lower [Fe²⁺] acts as the anode. Why does current flow in this cell?
To equalize the concentration of the Fe²⁺ ions, which increases system entropy






A galvanic cell is operating under non-standard conditions where the reaction quotient (Q) is greater than 1 (Q > 1). How does the cell potential (Ecell) compare to the standard cell potential (E°cell)?
Ecell < E°cell






The standard Gibbs free energy change (ΔG°) is related to the equilibrium constant (Keq) by the equation ΔG° = -RT ln(Keq). For a reaction where Keq ≫ 1 (products are favored at equilibrium), what is the sign of ΔG°?
ΔG° is negative






A battery is considered "dead" when it has reached equilibrium. What are the values of ΔG and Ecell for the reaction in a dead battery?
ΔG = 0, Ecell = 0






According to Faraday's law of electrolysis, which factor is directly proportional to the mass of a substance deposited at an electrode?
The current and the time (charge passed)






In a galvanic cell, electrons flow from the anode to the cathode. What is the direction of the conventional current?
From the cathode to the anode, opposite to the electron flow






The anode is the site of oxidation in both a Galvanic (G) and an Electrolytic (E) cell. How is the sign of the anode different in these two cell types, and why?
G: -; E: +. The spontaneity forces the sign change






The standard reduction potential for a half-reaction is +0.50 V. What is the standard oxidation potential for the reverse reaction?
-0.50 V
