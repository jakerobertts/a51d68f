Biology - The Cardiovascular System - MCAT Practice Questions

After leaving the right ventricle, blood immediately passes through which structure on its way to the pulmonary artery?
Pulmonary valve






Oxygenated blood returning from the pulmonary veins enters which chamber of the heart?
Left Atrium






The valve situated between the left atrium and the left ventricle is known as the:
Mitral valve






Which structure initiates the heartbeat and is referred to as the heart's natural pacemaker?
SA node






Which type of blood vessel is characterized by thick muscular walls and carries blood away from the heart?
Arteries






The blood traveling through the pulmonary veins is:
Oxygenated






Deoxygenated blood from the venae cavae first enters the:
Right atrium






Which part of the electrical conduction system transmits impulses from the AV node to the ventricles?
Bundle of His






The tricuspid valve is positioned between which two heart structures?
Right Atrium and Right Ventricle






Tiny branches of arteries that directly lead into the capillaries are called:
Arterioles






Capillaries are best suited for the diffusion of gases (O₂ and CO₂) and waste because their walls are:
1 cell thick






The blood found within the pulmonary arteries is typically:
Deoxygenated






Which component of the electrical conduction system carries the contraction impulse from the bundle branches to the myocardium of the ventricles?
Purkinje fibers






Blood leaving the left ventricle must pass through which valve before entering the aorta?
Aortic valve






Which blood vessels are generally characterized by thin walls, few elastic fibers, and carry blood toward the heart?
Veins
