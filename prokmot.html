Prokaryotic Organelles & Motility

Prokaryotic Cell Structures

  • The nucleoid is the region where the bacterial chromosome is located, not surrounded by a membrane.
  • Ribosomes in prokaryotes are 70S ribosomes that synthesize proteins throughout the cytoplasm.
  • The cell wall provides structural support and is composed primarily of peptidoglycan in bacteria.
  • Plasmids are small, circular DNA molecules that can replicate independently of the main chromosome.

    • Specialized Prokaryotic Structures

  • Pili are hair-like structures used for attachment to surfaces and DNA transfer during conjugation.
  • The capsule is a protective polysaccharide layer that surrounds some bacterial cells.
  • Inclusion bodies are storage granules containing nutrients, gases, or waste products.
  • Mesosomes are invaginations of the cell membrane that may play roles in DNA replication and cell division.

    • Prokaryotic Motility

  • Flagella are rotating structures that propel bacterial cells through liquid environments.
  • Chemotaxis is the directed movement of bacteria in response to chemical gradients.
  • Gliding motility allows some bacteria to move across surfaces without flagella.
  • Twitching motility is jerky movement powered by the extension and retraction of type IV pili.

    • Prokaryotic Reproduction

  • Binary fission is the primary method of reproduction in prokaryotes, producing identical daughter cells.
  • Conjugation is the transfer of genetic material between bacterial cells through direct contact.
  • Transformation is the uptake of free DNA from the environment by competent bacterial cells.
  • Transduction is the transfer of DNA between bacteria via bacteriophages (viruses).
