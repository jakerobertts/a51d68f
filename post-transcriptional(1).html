Post-transcriptional Modifications

  • Post-transcriptional modifications are crucial for the maturation of eukaryotic mRNA. These modifications include:
    • 5'capping, where the 5' end is modified with a 7-methylguanylate cap
    • Polyadenylation, which adds a poly(A) tail to the 3' end
    • Splicing, where introns are removed and exons are joined together
    • Alternative splicing, which allows for the production of multiple protein isoforms from a single gene.
    • Introns are thought to contribute to evolution by allowing for alternative splicing and the creation of new protein variants.
    • Ribozymes are RNA molecules that can catalyze their own splicing, playing a role in the removal of introns. Spliceosomes are large complexes made of RNA and protein that facilitate the splicing process by recognizing splice sites and catalyzing the removal of introns.
    • snRNPs (small nuclear ribonuclearproteins) are components of spliceosomes that recognize specific sequences at the intron-exon boundaries, guiding the splicing process.
    • snRNAs (small nuclear RNAs) are RNA molecules that are part of the spliceosome and play a crucial role in the splicing process by catalyzing the splicing reaction.
