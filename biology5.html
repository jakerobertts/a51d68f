Biology - The Respiratory System - MCAT Practice Questions

The complete airway pathway, from entry to gas exchange, is:
Nasal/oral cavities → pharynx → larynx → trachea → bronchi → bronchioles → alveoli






Which structure is a hollow, tubular structure often called the "voice box," and only air passes through it?
Larynx






The pharynx is commonly called the:
Throat






The trachea branches into two primary tubes called the:
Bronchi






Gas exchange in the lungs occurs specifically within the:
Alveoli






The smallest branches of the bronchi that end in the alveoli are the:
Bronchioles






Surfactant functions to:
Reduce surface tension in the alveoli






What is the function of the vibrissae?
Trapping large particles of dust in the nose






Ciliated epithelium in the trachea and bronchi work to move mucus and debris out of the respiratory tract, a process known as the:
Mucociliary escalator






The outside pleural membrane is known as the:
Parietal membrane






The fluid-filled space between the two pleurae is called the:
Pleural cavity (or space)






Inhalation is considered an active process because it requires the:
Contraction of the diaphragm and intercostal muscles






During exhalation, the diaphragm muscles:
Relax






The instrument used to measure the volume of air being exhaled or inhaled is a/an:
Spirometer






The amount of air that remains in a person's lungs after fully exhaling is the:
Residual volume






The maximum amount of air a person can expel from the lungs after a maximum inhalation is the:
Vital capacity






The volume of air inhaled and exhaled in a normal breath is known as the:
Tidal volume






The amount of air in the lungs after taking the deepest breath possible is the:
Total lung capacity






The volume of additional air that can be forcibly inhaled following a normal inhalation is the:
Inspiratory reserve volume






Which of the following is NOT typically measured by a standard spirometer?
Total lung capacity






The most important buffer for maintaining acid-base balance in the blood is the:
Bicarbonate buffer system






If the blood pH is too low (acidosis), respiration will:
Increase, blowing off CO₂






The primary respiratory control center is located in the:
Medulla oblongata






If the blood pH is too high (alkalosis), the respiratory response is to:
Decrease the breathing rate to trap more CO₂






The process of inhalation causes pressure in the thoracic cavity to:
Decrease, allowing air to rush in
