General Chemistry - Acids & Bases - MCAT Practice Questions

Which compound is typically classified as a Lewis acid?
BF₃






What is the Brønsted-Lowry base in the following equilibrium: HCO₃⁻(aq) + H₂O(l) ⇌ H₂CO₃(aq) + OH⁻(aq)?
H₂O






What is the conjugate base of the dihydrogen phosphate ion, H₂PO₄⁻?
HPO₄²⁻






Which characteristic most clearly distinguishes a Lewis acid from a Brønsted-Lowry acid?
It is an electron-pair acceptor






What term describes a substance that can react both as an acid and as a base, such as water?
Amphiprotic






A weak acid is characterized by what degree of dissociation in water?
Partial/Minimal dissociation






Given a solution with a pOH of 4.0 at 25°C, what is the pH of the solution?
10.0






If the concentration of [H⁺] in a solution is 1.0 × 10⁻⁵ M, what is the corresponding pH?
5.0






Which factor is the sole determinant of the Kw (ion-product constant for water)?
The temperature






If pKa for a weak acid is 9.2, what is the Ka value for this acid?
6.3 × 10⁻¹⁰






How is the strength of a weak acid related to the numerical value of its pKa?
Stronger acid has a smaller pKa






If 10 mL of a 0.1 M HCl solution is diluted to 100 mL, what is the new pH?
2.0






Which statement accurately describes a system that has reached dynamic chemical equilibrium?
The rate of the forward reaction equals the rate of the reverse reaction






How are the equilibrium constant (K) and the rate constants (kf and kr) for the forward and reverse reactions related?
K = kf / kr






What is the effect of increasing temperature on the equilibrium constant (K) for an endothermic reaction?
K increases






For the reaction A(g) + 2B(g) ⇌ C(g), what change will shift the equilibrium position toward the products?
Decrease the volume of the container






In the exothermic reaction: PCl₃(g) + Cl₂(g) ⇌ PCl₅(g) + Energy, what effect does adding a catalyst have on the amount of PCl₅ produced?
It has no effect on the final equilibrium yield






For which reaction system would increasing the volume of the container shift the equilibrium to the left?
The number of moles of gaseous reactants is less than products






If the reaction quotient (Q) for a given reaction is greater than the equilibrium constant (K), which direction will the reaction proceed?
Left (to form reactants)






What is the relationship between the equilibrium constant of an acid (Ka) and the equilibrium constant of its conjugate base (Kb) at 25°C?
Ka × Kb = Kw
