General Chemistry - Buffers & Titrations - MCAT Practice Questions

A Brønsted-Lowry base is a substance that:
Accepts H⁺






A Lewis acid is a substance that:
Accepts a pair of electrons






A species that can behave as an acid or a base is called:
Amphoteric species






Specific acids capable of losing more than a single proton per molecule are known as:
Polyprotic acids






Strong acids and bases are defined by the fact that they dissociate:
Completely






Weak acids and bases are characterized by the fact that they do not completely:
Dissociate






What is the equilibrium constant for the autoionization of water, Kw, at 25°C?
1.0 × 10⁻¹⁴






What is the sum of pH and pOH in an aqueous solution at 25°C?
14






The concentration of H⁺ is mathematically expressed as:
[H⁺] = 10⁻ᵖᴴ






The concentration of OH⁻ is mathematically expressed as:
[OH⁻] = 10⁻ᵖᴼᴴ






Ka is the equilibrium constant corresponding to the dissociation of an acid into a proton and its conjugate:
Base






Kb is the equilibrium constant corresponding to the dissociation of a base (BOH) into a hydroxide ion and its conjugate:
Acid






The formula for calculating pKa is:
pKa = -log(Ka)






What is the sum of pKa and pKb for a conjugate acid-base pair?
pKw






When choosing a titration indicator, you want to select one that changes color close to your:
Equivalence point






In an acid, what is the color change of blue litmus paper?
Changes to red






In a base, what is the color change of red litmus paper?
Changes to blue






In what type of solution do both red and blue litmus papers show no change in color?
Neutral






What color does universal litmus paper turn in a neutral solution?
Green






Phenolphthalein is colorless up to a pH of 8.2 and then changes color to:
Purple

4.4






Bromophenol blue is yellow up to a pH of 6 and is blue at any pH greater than:
8






The endpoint of a titration is defined as when:
The indicator reaches full color






Titrations of which type of substance show multiple buffering regions and equivalence points?
Polyvalent acid or base
