Gas Phase

  • The gas phase is one of the fundamental states of matter characterized by particles in constant, random motion with negligible intermolecular forces.
  • Kinetic molecular theory explains gas behavior through assumptions about particle motion, size, and interactions.
  • Absolute temperature is measured from absolute zero, the theoretical point where all molecular motion ceases.
  • The Kelvin scale is the absolute temperature scale used in gas law calculations, with 0 K = -273.15°C.
  • Pressure is the force exerted by gas molecules per unit area on the walls of their container.
  • A mercury barometer measures atmospheric pressure using the height of mercury supported by air pressure.
  • Molar volume is the volume occupied by one mole of any gas under specified conditions of temperature and pressure.
  • STP (Standard Temperature and Pressure) defines standard conditions where one mole of any ideal gas occupies 22.4 liters.
  • The ideal gas law relates pressure, volume, temperature, and amount of gas in the equation PV = nRT.
  • Boyle's law states that pressure and volume are inversely proportional at constant temperature and amount.
  • Charles's law states that volume and temperature are directly proportional at constant pressure and amount.
  • Avogadro's law states that equal volumes of gases at the same temperature and pressure contain equal numbers of molecules.
  • Heat capacity is the amount of energy required to raise the temperature of a substance by one degree.
  • The Boltzmann constant relates the average kinetic energy of particles to their absolute temperature.
  • Real gas behavior shows deviations from ideal gas law predictions due to intermolecular forces and finite molecular size.
  • The Van der Waals equation modifies the ideal gas law to account for intermolecular attractions and molecular volume.
  • Partial pressure is the pressure that one gas in a mixture would exert if it occupied the entire volume alone.
  • Mole fraction is the ratio of moles of one component to the total moles in a gas mixture.
  • Dalton's law states that the total pressure of a gas mixture equals the sum of the partial pressures of its components.
