Aldehydes and Ketones - MCAT Practice Questions

Amino acids are simple organic compounds containing both a carboxyl (—COOH) group and what other functional group?
Amino (—NH₂)






What term is used for hydrocarbon compounds containing carbon and hydrogen joined together in straight chains, branched chains, or non-aromatic rings?
Aliphatic






The C–N bond in a peptide bond has restricted rotation due to:
Resonance






A peptide bond is formed when the N-terminus of one amino acid nucleophilically attacks what part of another amino acid?
C-terminus






What basic nitrogen functional group contains a lone pair of electrons?
Amine






An amide functional group is characterized by a carbonyl (C=O) group linked to which atom?
Nitrogen






Polypeptides are large molecules made up of multiple amino acids linked by what type of bond?
Peptide






Which of the following is one of the two common laboratory methods for synthesizing amino acids?
Strecker synthesis






Strecker synthesis generates an amino acid starting from what precursor molecule?
Aldehyde






The Gabriel synthesis generates an amino acid from which set of reagents?
Potassium phthalimide, diethyl bromomalonate, and an alkyl halide






Phosphoric acid (H₃PO₄) is sometimes referred to as inorganic phosphate, which is commonly denoted as:
Pi






Phosphoric acid is an excellent buffer because it contains three hydrogens, each with:
A unique pKₐ






What bonds make up the backbone of DNA?
Phosphodiester bonds






Carbon-containing compounds that also have phosphate groups (like ATP and DNA) are classified as:
Organic phosphates






What is the defining feature of a carbonyl group?
A carbon-oxygen double bond






An aldehyde is a terminal functional group containing a carbonyl bonded to at least one:
Hydrogen






Which suffix is used in the IUPAC naming of aldehydes?
-al






In IUPAC naming, the aldehyde functional group is always given which numbering location?
1






A ketone is an internal functional group containing a carbonyl bonded to two:
Alkyl chains






Which prefixes can be used to denote the presence of a ketone in a molecule containing a higher priority functional group?
oxo- or keto-






Aldehydes and ketones are synthesized from alcohols through what type of reaction?
Oxidation






What weak oxidizing agent must be used to synthesize an aldehyde from a primary alcohol to prevent further oxidation to a carboxylic acid?
Pyridinium chlorochromate (PCC)






KMnO₄ (potassium permanganate) and H₂Cr₂O₄ (chromic acid) are classified as:
Strong oxidizing agents






Which is classified as a weak reducing agent that can reduce aldehydes and ketones, but not carboxylic acids or esters?
NaBH₄ (Sodium borohydride)






What strong reducing agent is needed to reduce a carboxylic acid or an ester into an alcohol?
LiAlH₄ (LAH)






Nucleophilic addition reactions involving a carbonyl group occur when a nucleophile attacks the slightly positive:
Carbon center






When water adds to a carbonyl group in a hydration reaction, what product is formed?
Geminal diol






The reaction of one equivalent of alcohol with an aldehyde forms a(n):
Hemiacetal






The reaction of two equivalents of alcohol with a ketone forms a(n):
Ketal






The reaction of nitrogen and nitrogen derivatives with carbonyls typically forms:
Imines






Imines can tautomerize to form which functional group?
Enamines






Hydrogen cyanide (HCN) reacts with carbonyls to form:
Cyanohydrins






The term "enol" comes from the fact that this functional group is an alkene that is also a(n):
Alcohol






The chemical equilibrium between a keto form and an enol form is known as:
Keto-enol tautomerization






The keto form is much more common than the enol form because the C=O double bond is significantly more stable (higher bond energy) than the:
C=C double bond






Removal of a proton from the less substituted α-carbon of a ketone gives the:
Kinetic enolate






Removal of a proton from the more substituted α-carbon of a ketone gives the:
Thermodynamic enolate






The product of an Aldol addition is an "aldol," which is a combination of what two functional groups?
Aldehyde and alcohol






The primary significance of an Aldol addition reaction is that it creates a new:
Carbon-carbon (C–C) bond






What reaction is the reverse of an Aldol reaction, typically catalyzed by heat, which cleaves the bond between the α- and β-carbon?
Retro-aldol reaction
