Biochemistry - Glycolysis - MCAT Practice Questions

Glycolysis breaks down one molecule of glucose into how many molecules of pyruvate?
Two molecules






What is the net ATP gain from glycolysis?
2 ATP






How many ATP molecules are consumed during the investment phase of glycolysis?
2 ATP






How many ATP molecules are produced during the payoff phase of glycolysis?
4 ATP






How many NADH molecules are produced in glycolysis?
2 NADH






Which enzyme catalyzes the first ATP-consuming step of glycolysis?
Hexokinase






Which enzyme catalyzes the second ATP-consuming step and is a major regulatory point in glycolysis?
Phosphofructokinase (PFK)






Which enzyme catalyzes the final ATP-producing step of glycolysis?
Pyruvate kinase






Which enzyme requires inorganic phosphate (Pi) and NAD⁺ and produces NADH in glycolysis?
Glyceraldehyde 3-phosphate dehydrogenase






Which enzyme produces water (H₂O) as a byproduct during glycolysis?
Enolase






Glucose is a molecule with how many carbon atoms?
6 carbons






Pyruvate is a molecule with how many carbon atoms?
3 carbons






Which enzyme catalyzes an ATP-producing step by converting ADP to ATP in the middle of glycolysis?
Phosphoglycerate kinase






Which of the following is NOT one of the three major regulatory enzymes in glycolysis?
Aldolase






How many total steps are involved in the glycolytic pathway?
10 steps
