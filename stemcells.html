Stem Cells & Differentiation

Stem Cell Types

  • Totipotent stem cells can differentiate into all cell types including extraembryonic tissues.
  • Pluripotent stem cells can differentiate into any cell type of the three germ layers.
  • Multipotent stem cells can differentiate into a limited number of related cell types.
  • Unipotent stem cells can only differentiate into one specific cell type.

    • Embryonic Development

  • Embryonic stem cells are derived from the inner cell mass of blastocysts.
  • Blastocyst is the hollow ball stage of embryonic development containing stem cells.
  • Inner cell mass is the cluster of cells inside the blastocyst that gives rise to the fetus.
  • Gastrulation is the process that establishes the three primary germ layers.

    • Germ Layers

  • Ectoderm gives rise to the nervous system, skin, and sensory organs.
  • Mesoderm forms muscles, bones, circulatory system, and reproductive organs.
  • Endoderm develops into the digestive tract, lungs, and associated organs.

    • Adult Stem Cells

  • Hematopoietic stem cells give rise to all types of blood and immune cells.
  • Mesenchymal stem cells can differentiate into bone, cartilage, fat, and other connective tissues.
  • Neural stem cells can produce neurons and glial cells in the nervous system.
  • Satellite cells are muscle stem cells responsible for muscle repair and regeneration.

    • Differentiation Process

  • Differentiation is the process by which cells become specialized for specific functions.
  • Morphogens are signaling molecules that provide positional information during development.
  • Transcription factors control gene expression programs that drive cell differentiation.
  • Epigenetics involves heritable changes in gene expression without DNA sequence changes.

    • Stem Cell Regulation

  • Stem cell niche is the specialized microenvironment that maintains stem cell properties.
  • Self-renewal is the ability of stem cells to divide while maintaining their undifferentiated state.
  • Asymmetric division produces one stem cell and one differentiated cell.
  • Quiescence is the dormant state that helps preserve stem cells for future use.

    • Induced Pluripotency

  • iPSCs are adult cells reprogrammed to become pluripotent stem cells.
  • Yamanaka factors are the four transcription factors used to create iPSCs.
  • Reprogramming is the process of converting one cell type into another.
  • Dedifferentiation is the reversal of cellular specialization back to a more primitive state.
