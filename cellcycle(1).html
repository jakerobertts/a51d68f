Cell Cycle & Division

Cell Cycle Phases

  • Interphase is the longest phase where the cell grows and prepares for division.
  • G1 phase is the first growth phase where cells increase in size and synthesize proteins.
  • S phase is when DNA replication occurs, duplicating the entire genome.
  • G2 phase is the second growth phase where cells prepare for mitosis.
  • M phase includes both mitosis and cytokinesis, resulting in cell division.

    • Cell Cycle Control

  • Checkpoints are quality control mechanisms that ensure proper cell division.
  • The G1/S checkpoint determines whether a cell is ready to enter S phase.
  • The G2/M checkpoint ensures DNA has been properly replicated before mitosis.
  • The spindle checkpoint ensures all chromosomes are properly attached before anaphase.

    • Cell Cycle Regulators

  • Cyclins are regulatory proteins whose levels fluctuate throughout the cell cycle.
  • CDKs are enzymes that phosphorylate target proteins to drive cell cycle progression.
  • p53 is a tumor suppressor protein that responds to DNA damage by halting the cell cycle.
  • Rb protein controls the G1/S checkpoint by regulating transcription factors.

    • Mitosis Stages

  • Prophase is when chromosomes condense and the nuclear envelope breaks down.
  • Metaphase is when chromosomes align at the cell's equator on the metaphase plate.
  • Anaphase is when sister chromatids separate and move to opposite poles of the cell.
  • Telophase is when nuclear envelopes reform around each set of chromosomes.
  • Cytokinesis is the physical division of the cytoplasm into two daughter cells.

    • Meiosis and Gametes

  • Meiosis is the process that produces gametes with half the chromosome number.
  • Crossing over is the exchange of genetic material between homologous chromosomes.
  • Independent assortment is the random distribution of chromosomes during meiosis.
  • Synapsis is the pairing of homologous chromosomes during prophase I of meiosis.
