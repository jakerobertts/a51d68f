Glycolysis & Pyruvate Processing

Overview

  • Glycolysis is the metabolic pathway that breaks down glucose into pyruvate, producing ATP and NADH.
  • Glycolysis occurs in the cytoplasm

Key Steps

  • Hexokinase phosphorylates glucose to glucose-6-phosphate using ATP.
  • Phosphofructokinase (PFK) is the rate-limiting enzyme and key regulatory step.
  • Pyruvate kinase catalyzes the final step, producing pyruvate and ATP.
